How to use machine learning for political questions

I want to demonstrate how polarised the two largest German parties, the SPD and the CDU/ CSU have been in the years since German reunification 1990. I will follow an approach by Peterson and Sperling (2018) to capture polarisation as the classification accuracy of a machine learning algorithm that tries to differentiate a legislative speech based on the party of the speaker. The idea is that higher polarisation will yield more different speeches and thus a higher accuracy of the classification.

I use the ParlSpeech dataset by Rauh and Schwalbach (2020), which contains a large amount of legislative speeches by German MPs over various previous legislative periods. I will show how polarisation levels have been changing over time by showing the accuracy with which a SVM classifier can predict the party of a speaker of a speech in the German Bundestag.

Classification accuracy (CA) is the share of correctly predicted groups by an algorithm that tries to differentiate between groups."

Just like a COVID-19 test, which you can evaluate by asking how often a test can correctly say when a person is infected or when he or she is not, CA regarding legislative speeches can tell us how often a classifier correclty predicts the party belonging to a speech. The more different speeches are, the easier it is for a classifier. And thus, the more different parties are.

Which is when we arrive at the matter of polarisation. But check for yourself.
